# 32 Brand Guidelines

## Name and product

- Divini Partners. An event-partnership marketplace operating system. Public positioning (V2): "Event Commerce Infrastructure."
- Part of the Divini Group family (sibling: Divini Procure). Capacitor bundle id: `com.divinigroup.divinipartners`.

## Visual identity

- Palette: deep emerald (`#123c2e` / `#1E5D4A`) with a champagne accent (`#D9CCB0`) on warm ivory/beige backgrounds (`#f3efe6`). Premium, editorial, boutique.
- Type: Cormorant Garamond (serif) for display/headings; Inter (sans) for body and UI.
- See `31_DESIGN_SYSTEM.md` for exact tokens.

## Voice and tone

- Confident, clear, and transparent, especially about money. The pricing story is a feature: vendors keep their full quote, clients see the fee, venues share in it.
- Premium but not stuffy; helpful and operational.
- Reposition away from "AI marketplace" and "subscription" language in public-facing copy; lead with infrastructure, commerce, and partnership.

## House writing rules

- No em dashes anywhere (a hard project convention enforced across code comments and docs). Use commas, parentheses, or separate sentences.
- Be exact with money: state the fee percentage, who pays it, and who receives the full amount.
- Avoid hype claims that overstate AI; describe concrete capability.

## Messaging pillars

1. Free to join, for every role.
2. Transparent on-top fee; vendors made whole.
3. Venues earn a share of every booking they host.
4. Intelligence that compounds with event history.
